Description: A volcanic eruption is the release of magma, gas, and ash from a volcano.

Impact: Can cause destruction to property, loss of life, and environmental changes; can lead to ashfall, lava flows, and pyroclastic flows.

Safety: Evacuate if instructed, stay indoors during ashfall, and wear masks to avoid inhaling ash.

Scale: Volcanic Explosivity Index (VEI) measures the explosiveness of eruptions on a scale from 0 to 8.

Recognition: Ash clouds, lava flows, and volcanic gases.

Formation: Occurs when magma from the Earth's mantle rises to the surface, often due to tectonic activity.
