üìñ

Description: An overflow of water that submerges land that is usually dry.

Recognition: Rising water levels, heavy rainfall, and overflowing rivers.

Scale: No formal scale, but categorized by severity (minor, moderate, major).

Formation: Caused by heavy rainfall, rapid snowmelt, or dam failure.

Impact: Can cause property damage, loss of life, and disruption of services.

Types: Flash floods, river floods, coastal floods, and urban flooding.

Safety: Move to higher ground, avoid driving through floodwaters, and listen to emergency alerts.
