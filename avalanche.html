Avalanche

üìñ Definitions

Description: A rapid flow of snow down a slope, often triggered by natural or human activity.

Impact: Can bury structures, block roads, and pose a threat to life.

Safety: Avoid avalanche-prone areas, carry safety equipment, and follow local warnings.

Scale: No formal scale, but categorized by size and type (slab, loose snow).

Recognition: Loud rumbling noise, snow clouds, and rapid movement of snow.

Formation: Occurs when a layer of snow becomes unstable, often due to warming, rain, or human activity.

Types: Slab avalanches, loose snow avalanches, and wet snow avalanches.
